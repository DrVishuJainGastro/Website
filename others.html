Conditions Treated

  • Corrosive injuries : Injuries caused by ingestion of corrosive substances, such as acids or alkalis.

  • Shunt surgery : A surgical procedure to reroute blood flow in the liver, often performed to treat portal hypertension or liver cirrhosis.


Symptoms to Look Out For

Corrosive Injuries
  • Severe pain in the mouth, throat, or chest
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Drooling
  • Vomiting
  • Burning sensation in the mouth, throat, or chest
  • Blood in vomit or saliva
Shunt Surgery
  • Enlarged veins in the abdomen (varices)
  • Bleeding from varices
  • Ascites (fluid buildup in the abdomen)
  • Jaundice
  • Confusion or altered mental status
